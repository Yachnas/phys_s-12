Molding


For the first part of the assignment I chose to mold a lego. I wanted to create a lego mold so that I could cast a chocolate lego brick. To prepare for molding I found a ventilated area and put on vinyl gloves and my safety goggles. For the mold container I used one of the provided plastic cups in the kit molding and casting bag.



With my materials and safety equipment ready I began to create the mold. Since the plastic cup I was using was already waterproof, I did not need to cover it with tape or anything so I used it as is. I taped the bottom of the lego brick to the bottom of the plastic food package with a piece of double sided tape.



Afterwards I started preparing the silicon mixture. I slowly stirred both the solutions for about three minutes and was careful not to let any new air bubbles form. I then poured the white solution to about 25 CC in the provided cup, before pouring the purple solution to 50 CC.



I decided to use a lot less of the two solutions because I did not think that I would need a lot since I was only planning to mold a single lego brick in an already small container. I stirred the white and purple solution for another three minutes until white streaks were no longer visible and the solution looked uniform. After the mixture was complete I slowly poured it into the prepared container. I poured the mixture over a single area of the container and avoided drizzling it over the entirety of the surface so that I did not miss any air bubbles. Once the lego brick was submerged in the solution I quickly shook the container side to side to get rid of any final remaining air bubbles.



The next day I observed the mold and found that the solution hardened.



I used an exacto knife and my fingers to carefully pry the solution from the container. The mold turned out great, and I could see that the details of the lego brick were clearly visible in the mold.



Casting


With the molding portion finished I began on the casting part. I found some chocolate and melted it in the microwave. I took the melted chocolate and slowly poured it into the lego mold. After I poured the melted chocolate in I tried scraping the excess off the mold and waited a couple of minutes for it to cool. It was taking way longer than I thought it would to cool so I ended up putting it in the freezer.



After the chocolate cooled I removed it from the mold. I cut off the excess chocolate that stuck out from the lego shape. I was really happy with the final result. It looked exactly like a normal lego but made out of chocolate instead of plastic. I could probably use this lego mold in the future to create chocolate legos for pancakes.
